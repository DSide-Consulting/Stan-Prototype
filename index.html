A quick movement test

Try this. Then tell us what changed.

Standing, hands on hips. Slowly arch backward five times — only as far as feels reasonable. Pause. Then look at where your symptoms are now.

What we're looking for: changes in where the pain is, not just whether it's better or worse. Pain moving toward the spine — even if intensity is similar — is a powerful diagnostic signal.

Your advisor

McGill / Biomechanics

Stuart McGill's framework finds the specific load the spine doesn't tolerate, eliminates that trigger, and builds stability around it. Based on your chair test, you have a clear, actionable pattern.

Discogenic pattern • Flexion-intolerant
Your pain is reproducibly triggered by spinal flexion under load — slouching, sitting, bending, lifting with rounded back. The treatment is the inverse: eliminate the trigger, build stability with the Big 3, restore tolerance to load over weeks.

Named pain trigger

Slouched sitting under load — chair test, limb 2. This is what you avoid. Spine hygiene is built around it.

Spine hygiene rules

  • Sneeze brace. Brace your core before sneezing or coughing. Don't take a sneeze in flexion.
  • Hip-hinge for lifts. Anything off the floor is a hip-hinge, not a back-bend. Knees bend; spine stays neutral.
  • Neutral-spine sit. No prolonged slouching. Lumbar support, breaks every 30 minutes.

Precautions

No flexion under load No sit-ups or crunches Stop if leg symptoms appear
Your advisor

McKenzie MDT

Robin McKenzie's framework classifies most mechanical low-back pain into three categories. Based on your answers, you fit a pattern with very good prognosis.

Reducible Derangement
The most common mechanical pattern, and the one with the strongest evidence for self-directed care. Centralization tells us the right direction. Repeated end-range loading in that direction is the treatment.

What this means for you

  • Direction matters. Extension-biased movement is what your spine wants right now. Flexion (rounding forward, prolonged sitting) is likely making it worse.
  • Frequency over intensity. Many small doses through the day beat one long session.
  • Watch the signal. If symptoms move farther from your spine during a movement, stop and we'll reassess.

Precautions

Avoid prolonged sitting No flexion under load Stop if leg symptoms worsen
